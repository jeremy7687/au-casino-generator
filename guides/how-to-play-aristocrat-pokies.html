Reel Power vs Paylines Explained

One of Aristocrat's most significant technical innovations is the Reel Power system — a mechanic that replaces traditional fixed paylines with a "ways-to-win" engine. Instead of needing symbols to land on specific lines, Reel Power pays whenever matching symbols appear on adjacent reels from left to right, regardless of their vertical position. This fundamentally changes how you size your bets and understand winning potential.

In a standard 243-ways Aristocrat pokie, your total bet covers all ways simultaneously. There are no individual payline adjustments — you simply choose your coin value and the number of coins per way (usually 1–5). This means a $1.00 spin on a 243-ways game covers far more winning combinations than a $1.00 spin on a 25-line fixed game, which is why punters find Reel Power titles feel more action-packed at equivalent bet sizes.

Xtra Reel Power titles like Buffalo expand the grid to 4 rows (instead of 3), creating 1,024 ways. The bet sizing scales proportionally — minimum spins on Buffalo typically start around $0.50 AUD rather than the $0.01 you might stake on a traditional payline game.
