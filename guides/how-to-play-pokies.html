How Pokies Work

Before you place your first bet, understanding the mechanics puts you in a far stronger position. Four core concepts govern every pokie you'll ever spin — and each one directly affects your bankroll.

📊
Typical Range: 94–97%
RTP — Return to Player

RTP is the percentage of total wagered money a pokie pays back to punters over millions of spins. A 96% RTP means for every $100 wagered, the game theoretically returns $96. It's calculated over huge sample sizes, not individual sessions — so short-term variance is huge. Always choose pokies with RTP above 95% for the best long-run odds. High RTP does not mean you'll win 96% back in one sitting, but it does reduce the house edge over time.

🌊
Low / Medium / High
Volatility

Low volatility pokies pay smaller amounts frequently — good for casual punters stretching a small deposit. Medium volatility balances frequency and size. High volatility pokies pay infrequently but can deliver massive wins — ideal for punters chasing big sessions with a healthy bankroll. Most fan-favourite titles like Gates of Olympus and Sweet Bonanza run high volatility with multiplier potential into the thousands.

🔢
Fixed / Megaways / Cluster
Paylines & Ways to Win

Fixed paylines (e.g. 20 lines) pay when matching symbols land left to right. 243 Ways pay whenever matching symbols appear on adjacent reels regardless of position. Megaways engines (developed by BTG) create up to 117,649 ways on every spin by randomising reel height. Cluster pays (used in Reactoonz) win when a set number of matching symbols connect in a cluster, eliminating paylines altogether.

⚡
Bonus Round Triggers
Bonus Features

Modern pokies are defined by their feature sets. Free spins are the most common bonus — typically triggered by 3+ scatter symbols. Multipliers amplify wins by 2x, 5x, even 100x+. Sticky wilds lock in place across re-spins, dramatically boosting hit rate. Pick bonus rounds let you choose items to reveal prizes. Buy-a-bonus features (where available) let you purchase instant free spins entry — useful on a limited session.

Smart Bankroll Tips for Online Pokies

Pokies are entertainment first. These five principles keep the fun going longer and protect your balance from the inevitable variance spikes every high-volatility session brings.

  • Set a hard session budget before you open the casino. Decide what you're comfortable losing entirely — treat it as the cost of entertainment. Once it's gone, close the tab. Never top up mid-session chasing losses; that's where bankrolls collapse fast.
  • Match volatility to your bankroll size. High-volatility pokies like Gates of Olympus can go 200+ spins without a meaningful win. If your session budget is under $50, play medium or low volatility titles where your balance survives long enough to hit bonus rounds. Save the high-volatility machines for bigger deposit days.

Pokies are designed to take back winnings over time; the RTP is calculated over millions of spins, not individual sessions. Banking a win and walking away is the closest thing to a "strategy" that genuinely works.

Activate them. They're not just for problem gamblers; they're smart session management tools that prevent the "just five more spins" spiral on a losing run.
