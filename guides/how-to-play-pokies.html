Game Mechanics

How Online Pokies Work

Before you stake real money, it pays to understand the core mechanics behind every online pokie. RTP, volatility, paylines, and bonus features all directly affect your experience — and your bankroll.

RTP

Return to Player (RTP)

RTP is the percentage of all wagered money a pokie pays back over millions of spins. A 96% RTP means the game theoretically returns $96 for every $100 wagered. For real money online pokies in Australia, target titles with 96%+ RTP — significantly better than land-based machines averaging 87–90%.

Volatility

Volatility & Variance

Volatility describes the risk and reward pattern of a pokie:

  • Low Frequent small wins. Best for casual punters on tight budgets. Long sessions, steady bankroll.
  • Medium Balanced mix. Good all-rounder for most punters. Mix of small and occasional big wins.
  • High Rare but massive wins. Best for punters with bigger bankrolls who can handle dry spells.
Paylines

Paylines & Ways to Win

Modern online pokies use several win structures beyond classic fixed paylines:

  • Fixed Paylines — Set number of lines (e.g. 20, 25, 50) always active.
  • Megaways™ — Up to 117,649 ways to win per spin. Dynamic reel sizes. Huge variance.
  • Cluster Pays — Win by landing clusters of matching symbols. No paylines at all.
  • 243/1024 Ways — Fixed ways across all reels, no specific lines required.
Features

Bonus Features Explained

Bonus features are where the real excitement (and payouts) happen:

  • Free Spins — Extra spins at no cost, often with multipliers attached.
  • Sticky Wilds — Wild symbols that lock in place for multiple spins.
  • Multipliers — Multiply wins by 2x, 5x, 100x or more during features.
  • Bonus Buy — Pay to directly enter the bonus round. Not all casinos allow this.
  • Jackpots — Fixed or progressive prize pools. Mega Moolah can exceed $10M AUD.
💡

Pro Tip: RTP is a Long-Run Average

RTP percentages are calculated over millions of spins — not your single session. A 96% RTP pokie can pay out 300x in one session and nothing in the next. Use RTP to compare games, not to predict session outcomes. Combine it with volatility to find the right match for your bankroll style.
