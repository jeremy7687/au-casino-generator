Mechanics

How Online Pokies Work

Every online pokie is powered by a Random Number Generator (RNG) — software that generates thousands of random outcomes per second. When you hit spin, the RNG locks in a result that determines where the reels stop. The outcome is completely independent of your previous spins, your bet size, and how long you've been playing. There is no memory, no hot or cold streaks — just maths and probability.

Understanding four key mechanics will help you choose the right pokies and manage your sessions smarter:

📊

RTP (Return to Player)

RTP is the theoretical percentage of wagered money a pokie returns to punters over millions of spins. A 96% RTP means the game returns $96 for every $100 wagered — over the long run. Most online pokies sit between 94% and 97% RTP. Always check the paytable info before playing. Higher RTP = better odds for the punter.

Target: 96%+
📈

Volatility

Low volatility: frequent small wins — good for stretching a small bankroll and casual sessions. Medium volatility: balanced risk/reward — the most popular choice. High volatility: rare but large wins — suited to punters with bigger bankrolls who want a shot at massive payouts. Know your session goal before choosing.

Match to bankroll
🎰

Paylines & Ways to Win

Classic pokies have fixed paylines (10, 20, 25). Modern games use 243 ways to win, cluster pays (symbols connect in groups), or the industry-changing Megaways engine — which can generate up to 117,649 ways to win on each spin based on variable reel heights.

Megaways = max action
⚡

Bonus Features

Modern pokies are loaded with features: Free Spins rounds triggered by scatter symbols, Multipliers that compound wins, Sticky Wilds that lock in place for multiple spins, Bonus Rounds that launch mini-games, and Buy Bonus options that let you purchase direct entry to the feature for a set cost.

Features = big potential

Smart Play

5 Bankroll Tips for Online Pokies

Pokies are a form of entertainment with a built-in house edge. These five principles won't flip the maths in your favour, but they will make your sessions last longer, feel more controlled, and occasionally deliver a proper result:

🎯

Set a Hard Session Limit Before You Log In

Decide your maximum spend before you open the casino. Whether it's $20 or $200, treat it as an entertainment budget — not an investment. When it's gone, the session ends. No chasing. This single habit separates casual punters from those who develop problems.

💡

Match Volatility to Your Bankroll

High-volatility pokies like Wanted Dead or a Wild or Money Train 4 can go 200+ spins without a bonus trigger — that's brutal on a $20 bankroll. Save high-volatility titles for sessions when you have 150+ spins at your chosen bet size. Use low-to-medium volatility for smaller bankrolls.

🏆

Bank a Percentage of Any Big Win Immediately

If you hit a win that's 20x your spin bet or more, consider withdrawing 50% of your total balance immediately. Playing with "house money" is psychologically easier, and you lock in a real win rather than spinning it all back — which statistics show most punters do.

🔍

Always Use Bonus Offers — But Read the Terms

Casino bonuses give you extra funds to play with, extending your session time. However, wagering requirements (e.g. 35x the bonus) mean you need to wager the bonus amount many times before withdrawing. Prioritise low wagering or no wagering requirement bonuses for real value. Cashback bonuses are often the most punter-friendly offer available.

⏱️

Take Breaks — Autoplay Isn't Always Your Friend

Autoplay can drain your balance faster than manual spinning because it removes the natural pauses in play. Set a manual spin count limit if you use autoplay, or simply spin manually. Regular breaks help you stay aware of how your session is actually going rather than letting the reels blur into background noise.
