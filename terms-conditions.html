4.7

Australian Gambling Law

The regulation of online gambling in Australia is primarily governed by the Interactive Gambling Act 2001 (Cth) ("IGA"), as amended by the Interactive Gambling Amendment Act 2017. Under the IGA, it is prohibited for operators to provide certain interactive gambling services (including real-money online casino games) to Australian residents without authorisation.

However, it is important to understand the following:

  • The IGA places restrictions on operators, not on individual players. There is no law in Australia that makes it illegal for an individual to access or use a licensed offshore online casino.
  • Many Australians legally access online casino services offered by operators licensed in offshore jurisdictions such as Curaçao, Malta (MGA), Gibraltar, Isle of Man, and similar reputable regulatory bodies.
  • Casinos listed on AussiePokies96 are licensed and regulated in offshore jurisdictions and accept Australian players where they are permitted to do so.
  • AussiePokies96 does not claim, represent, or imply that any listed casino holds an Australian-issued licence, ACMA approval, or authorisation from any Australian government body.
